Bridge and Structural Engineering Services

Polaris Bridge Engineering LLC provides focused engineering support for transportation infrastructure, bridge programs, design-build teams, contractors, and facility owners. Services can be delivered as a defined task, a subconsultant assignment, supplemental project capacity, or construction-phase support.

Bridge Design & Rehabilitation

Polaris provides analysis, design, and plan-development support for new bridges, replacements, widenings, and rehabilitation projects. Experience includes steel plate girder, prestressed concrete girder, continuous composite, cast-in-place concrete, culvert, substructure, and foundation systems.

Typical assignments

  • New bridge, replacement, and widening design
  • Bridge rehabilitation and retrofit design
  • Deck, bearing, and expansion-joint replacement
  • Steel section repair and strengthening
  • Concrete repair, crack repair, and durability improvements
  • Abutment, pier, footing, pile, and drilled-shaft design
  • Curved, skewed, staged, and continuous bridge analysis
  • Design calculations, plan development, specifications, quantity support, and independent technical review

Applicable standards include AASHTO LRFD Bridge Design Specifications, owner criteria, and state DOT manuals.

Load Rating & Inspection Support

Polaris supports bridge owners and prime consultants with load-rating analysis, inspection-related engineering, and condition assessment. Deliverables are organized to be traceable, reviewable, and consistent with owner procedures.

Typical assignments

  • LRFR and LFR load-rating analysis
  • AASHTOWare Bridge Rating (BrR) modeling and documentation
  • As-built or inventory load-rating updates
  • Posting evaluation and special-vehicle review
  • Bridge condition assessment and repair prioritization
  • Inspection team-leader or engineering support, subject to project staffing requirements
  • Railroad bridge evaluation using AREMA criteria
  • Independent load-rating review and quality-control support

Applicable standards include AASHTO Manual for Bridge Evaluation, NBIS requirements, AREMA criteria, and owner-specific procedures.

Construction Engineering & Temporary Works

Polaris helps contractors and design-build teams plan and execute complex bridge construction activities by evaluating load paths, temporary conditions, staged operations, and constructability.

Typical assignments

  • Bridge jacking, blocking, and bearing-replacement support
  • Temporary shoring, support towers, falsework, and temporary framing
  • Erection-stage and staged-construction analysis
  • Constructability review and value-engineering support
  • Shop drawing, working drawing, and submittal review
  • Field-condition evaluation and repair-detail development
  • Construction sequencing and temporary load-path assessment
  • Construction-phase responses and design clarification

Construction-engineering scopes are developed around the contractor's means, methods, sequence, equipment, access, and project-specific safety requirements.

Structural Assessment & Facility Engineering

Polaris applies bridge-level structural judgment to transportation facilities, commercial buildings, and site structures where existing conditions, modifications, or repair needs require focused engineering evaluation.

Typical assignments

  • Structural condition assessments and engineering reports
  • Steel, concrete, and foundation repair recommendations
  • Rooftop equipment support framing and existing-roof capacity checks
  • Structural modifications for renovations and tenant improvements
  • Retaining walls, site structures, and transportation-related facilities
  • Parking structure and airport facility assessment support
  • Damage assessment, cause evaluation, and repair design
